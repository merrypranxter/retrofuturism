# Retrofuturism Taxonomy

## Primary family tree

1. **Industrial proto-retrofutures**
   - Victorian steampunk
   - Clockwork-adjacent neo-Victorian futures
2. **Machine-age state and infrastructure futures**
   - Raygun gothic pulp futures
   - Interwar dieselpunk
   - Soviet cosmist retrofutures
3. **Postwar consumer-space futures**
   - Atompunk / space-age pop
   - Space race brutalist functionalism
4. **Late analog techno-retrofutures**
   - Cassette futurism
   - Retro anime mecha modernism
5. **Millennial digital-retrofutures**
   - Y2K retrofuture pop

## Taxonomy boundaries (to avoid blur)

- **Raygun gothic vs atompunk**
  - Raygun gothic is pre-Apollo pulp-theatrical and illustrator-led.
  - Atompunk is postwar consumer-industrial and ad-design-led.
- **Dieselpunk vs space-race brutalism**
  - Dieselpunk relies on ideological monumentality and streamline forms.
  - Space-race brutalism emphasizes engineering pragmatics and mission realism.
- **Cassette futurism vs retro anime mecha**
  - Cassette futurism is equipment-centric and utilitarian.
  - Retro anime mecha adds stylized narrative framing and mech-specific grammar.
- **Y2K retrofuture vs cyberpunk**
  - Y2K retrofuture is optimistic, glossy, and brand-mediated.
  - Cyberpunk is anti-corporate, decayed, and socially antagonistic.
