# Glossary

- **Adjacency drift:** Unintended slide from one style family into another due to shared motifs.
- **Analog legibility:** Degree to which controls, labels, and physical interfaces remain interpretable without touch-screen assumptions.
- **Composition grammar:** Reusable camera/framing structures that reinforce a style's worldview.
- **Decofuturism:** Future-oriented interpretation of Art Deco geometry and ornament systems.
- **Diegetic interface:** Interface elements that exist inside the world (panels, dials, printed overlays).
- **Ornament logic:** Rules governing where, why, and how decoration appears.
- **Retrofuture:** A vision of the future constructed from historical design assumptions.
- **Shape language:** Family of geometric forms that defines silhouette identity.
- **Style collapse:** Loss of distinction between substyles due to generic descriptors.
- **Symbol system:** Cohesive set of emblems/icons tied to social function and narrative meaning.
- **Texture hierarchy:** Structured distribution of macro/meso/micro surface detail.
- **Variant fidelity:** Accuracy in representing regional or historical sub-variants.
