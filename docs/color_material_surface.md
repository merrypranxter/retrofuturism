# Color, Material, and Surface Logic

## Palette strategies

- **Industrial heat palette:** brass, umber, soot black (steampunk).
- **Metal-state palette:** gunmetal, concrete gray, warning red (diesel/space-race).
- **Optimist consumer palette:** cyan, coral, lemon, white (atompunk).
- **Nocturnal analog palette:** navy, charcoal, amber accents, CRT cyan (cassette).
- **Gloss techno-pop palette:** violet, aqua, neon-yellow highlights (Y2K).

## Material authenticity guide

| Material family | Styles where primary | Behavior cues |
|---|---|---|
| Brass/copper alloys | Victorian steampunk | Tarnish, etched ornament, warm speculars |
| Painted steel + Bakelite | Dieselpunk, cassette | Chips on edges, utilitarian color-coding |
| Fiberglass + laminate | Atompunk | Soft gloss, pastel-compatible reflections |
| Concrete + aluminum | Space-race brutalist, Soviet cosmist | Chalky diffuse concrete + cool metallic trim |
| Polycarbonate + chrome plastic | Y2K retrofuture pop | High reflectance, translucent color bleed |

## Surface aging rules

- Styles with engineering realism should show localized wear near access points.
- Consumer-oriented optimistic styles may be intentionally low-wear.
- Aging must match narrative context (museum retro, active service hardware, archival reconstruction).

## Contrast control

- Use **structural contrast** (mass vs void) for monumental styles.
- Use **interface contrast** (lit displays vs dark housing) for analog tech styles.
- Use **chromatic contrast** (clean color blocks) for ad-driven styles.
